🏝️ Kedah, Malaysia

Langkawi

A duty-free archipelago of 99 islands draped in ancient rainforest, white sandy shores, and crystal Andaman waters.

🌤️
Best Time
Nov – Apr
✈️
Airport
Langkawi Intl
🏷️
Budget
RM150–600/night
🌡️
Temperature
26–33°C

Langkawi SkyCab

One of the world's steepest cable cars, soaring 708m above sea level over Mount Mat Cincang with breathtaking jungle and sea views.

Pantai Cenang

The island's most popular beach — a long stretch of golden sand lined with cafés, watersports, and glorious sunsets.

Kilim Geoforest Park

A UNESCO Geopark featuring mystical mangroves, limestone caves, and eagle feeding — best explored by boat tour.

Durian Perangin Waterfall

A hidden jungle gem with 14 cascades tumbling down rocky granite — refreshing and serene, perfect for nature lovers.

Cameron Highlands

Malaysia's most beloved highland escape — emerald tea fields, cool breezes, and a serene pace of life at 1,500m above sea level.

BOH Tea Plantations

Walk among endless rows of manicured tea bushes, tour the factory, and sip freshly brewed tea with panoramic valley views.

Strawberry Farms

Pick your own fresh strawberries straight from the vines — a fun and delicious activity for all ages.

Mossy Forest, Gunung Brinchang

At 2,032m, the highest peak you can drive to — a mystical misty forest draped with ancient moss, ferns, and gnarled trees.

Lavendar Garden

A small garden with lavender & other colourful blooms,plus a cafe and restaurant.

Redang Island

One of Malaysia's nine marine parks — home to impossibly clear water, coral gardens, and the best sea turtle sightings on the east coast.

🌤️
Best Time
Mar – Oct
⛴️
Ferry from KT
45-min ferry
🏷️
Budget
RM200–700/night
🌡️
Water Temp
27–29°C

Snorkelling & Diving

Redang's waters boast over 30m visibility — swim among parrotfish, clownfish, and green sea turtles in pristine coral gardens.

Teluk Dalam Beach

A powdery white crescent beach with calm shallow waters — ideal for swimming, kayaking, and total relaxation.

Marine Park Visitors Centre

Learn about Redang's marine ecosystem, conservation efforts, and sea turtle nesting sites.

Night Snorkelling

An otherworldly experience — glide over bioluminescent plankton and discover nocturnal sea creatures in phosphorescent water.

🏔️ Borneo, Malaysia

Sabah

The land below the wind — towering peaks, pristine coral reefs, ancient rainforests, and encounters with wildlife found nowhere else on Earth.

🌤️
Best Time
Apr – Sep
✈️
Gateway
Kota Kinabalu Intl
🏷️
Budget
RM120–500/night
🌡️
Temperature
24–34°C

Mount Kinabalu

Southeast Asia's highest peak at 4,095m — a UNESCO World Heritage Site and bucket-list summit for trekkers worldwide.

Sipadan Island

Ranked among the world's top dive sites — walls of barracuda, schools of bumphead parrotfish, and resident hammerhead sharks.

Sepilok Orangutan Sanctuary

Watch Borneo's iconic orangutans at the world-famous rehabilitation centre — an unforgettable wildlife experience.

Kinabatangan River Safari

Cruise the longest river in Sabah to spot Bornean pygmy elephants, proboscis monkeys, and hornbills.

🍜 George Town, Malaysia

Penang

The Pearl of the Orient — a living UNESCO heritage city where colonial grandeur, vibrant street art, and unbeatable food collide.

🌤️
Best Time
Year-round
✈️
Airport
Penang Intl (PEN)
🏷️
Budget
RM80–400/night
🌡️
Temperature
25–34°C

George Town Heritage Zone

A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2008 — wander lanes of shophouses, clan jetties, mosques, temples, and colonial mansions.

Street Art Trail

Ernest Zacharevic's iconic murals transformed Penang into an open-air gallery — interactive, witty, and endlessly photogenic.

Penang Hill (Bukit Bendera)

Ride the funicular to 830m for panoramic views over the island, sea, and mainland — especially magical at sunset.

Kek Lok Si Temple

Southeast Asia's largest Buddhist temple — a grand complex of pagodas, statues, and lantern-lit pathways on the hillside.
